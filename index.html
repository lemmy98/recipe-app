Nigerian Fried Rice Recipe

Nigerian Fried Rice is a colorful, flavorful dish made with parboiled rice stir-fried with vegetables, seasonings, and often liver or shrimp. Its a staple at Nigerian parties and pairs beautifully with fried chicken or grilled meats.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 30 minutes
  • Servings: 4–6
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

Ingredients

  • Parboiled rice (2 cups)
  • Mixed vegetables (carrots, green beans, peas, sweet corn)
  • Chicken stock (2–3 cups)
  • Seasoning cubes or powder
  • Curry powder and thyme
  • Vegetable oil
  • Chicken liver or shrimp (optional)

Instructions

  1. Parboil or pre-cook the rice in seasoned chicken stock until it is just slightly undercooked. Set aside.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat vegetable oil and sauté chicken liver or shrimp (if using) until cooked. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add more oil if needed, then stir-fry the mixed vegetables for a few minutes until slightly tender but still crisp.
  4. Add the rice into the pan in small portions, seasoning with curry, thyme, and seasoning cubes as you mix.
  5. Stir-fry everything together on medium-high heat for several minutes.
  6. adding the cooked liver or shrimp back in. Adjust seasoning and serve hot.

Tips

  • For the best texture, the rice should be firm—not soft—before stir-frying.
  • Curry powder gives the signature color and aroma, so don’t skip it.
  • You can add bell peppers, onions, or diced green peppers for extra flavor.
  • Stir-fry in batches to prevent the rice from becoming mushy.
